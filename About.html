1. Groundwater Prospect Mapping (1:10 K)

PREAMBLE

Groundwater plays an important role in increasing food and agricultural production, providing safe drinking water and facilitating industrial development in India. It contributes fresh water to meet the requirement of nearly 65% of total irrigated area, nearly 85% of the rural drinking water supply and 50% of the urban drinking water needs of the country. Over the last three decades, the rapid expansion in the use of groundwater, primarily for irrigation, has contributed significantly to its agricultural production and overall economic development. This has also resulted in India becoming the largest ground water extractor in the World.

Maharashtra is a progressive State with a positive track record of development in all sectors like infrastructure, irrigation, industry, agriculture, Water Supply and Sanitation etc. Remote Sensing, GIS and GPS along with the latest technologies of DRONES (UAV) and LiDAR are being used to provide timely and accurate inputs for better decision support and e-Governance.

Maharashtra Remote Sensing Application Centre (MRSAC) and Groundwater Surveys and Development Agency (GSDA) has successfully completed the prestigious project of Rajeev Gandhi National Drinking Water Mission (RGNDWM) sponsored by Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), Government of India. Subsequently the groundwater prospect database is being shared with many departments of Government of Maharashtra for rural development. The projects like RS & GIS based mapping for water supply and Sanitation (WSS) using High Resolution Satellite Data for Mapping and monitoring of rural drinking water supply schemes and Prediction of Water Scarcity Zones and Droughts using GIS Decision Support Tool (DSS) on WEBGIS for Maharashtra State for Real time groundwater scarcity prediction are also carried out by MRSAC along with the expertise and domain knowledge of Groundwater Surveys and Development Agency (GSDA).

In this connection, MRSAC is generating GIS database for hydrological and hydrogeological assets like water bodies, irrigation wells, surface and groundwater drinking water sources, groundwater prospect zonation both in terms of quantity and quality, land use land cover and existing water and soil conservation structures using high resolution satellite data. This geospatial database is being hosted on a Geoportal with dashboard facility for providing maps to the users for preparing scientific Village Water Security Plan.

2. NEED FOR VILLAGE LEVEL GROUNDWATER PROSPECTS MAP

Ground water is the major source for all purposes of water requirements in India. More than 90% of rural and nearly 30% of urban population depend on it for drinking water. The dependency on the ground water is increasing due to increase in population. In recent years, over-exploitation of groundwater, particularly for irrigation, has resulted in significant depletion of water tables. In the over-exploited zones, the wells are getting dried up in due course of time. In some of the areas, the situation is so serious that there is a scarcity of water even for drinking purposes. In these areas, the ground water needs to be recharged artificially, as the natural recharge is not sufficient, to augment the resource and maintain the water table. On the other hand, the resource is not yet exploited to the optimum level in many areas in the country. Also, since few years various parts of the country are facing severe drought, around 68% of the country is prone to drought in varying degrees. Currently available groundwater prospects map have their limitations and scope is also limited. So, there is requirement for updating 1:50,000 scale ground water prospects map to 1:2,000 scale to meet the present-day requirements especially at village level.

3. BACKGROUND FOR PREPARATION OF GROUNDWATER PROSPECTS MAPS

Identification of potable ground water sources which are sustainable for longer periods is a critical issue in the supply of drinking water to the rural habitations in the country. To address this issue, the erstwhile Department of Drinking Water and Sanitation (DDWS), Ministry of Rural Development (MoRD), Govt. of India which is the apex organization for drinking water supply in the country has requested National Remote Sensing Centre (NRSC), ISRO / Dept. of Space, Govt. of India to prepare ground water prospects maps (technically Hydro Geo Morphological-HGM maps) on 1:50,000 scale using remote sensing and GIS technology. The Ministry wants these maps to be used by the engineers and hydro geologists of the line departments in respective states for identifying ground water sources, particularly for Not Covered (NC) and Partially Covered (PC) habitations. NRSC has taken up the work in phased manner under National Rural Drinking Water Programme (NRDWP) project. The maps have already been prepared for the country and handed over to the line departments in respective states and hosted in Bhuvan Bhujal portal (http://www.nrsc.gov.in/Bhuvan-Bhujal).

4. OBJECTIVES

MRSAC and GSDA have successfully completed the prestigious project of Rajeev Gandhi National Drinking Water Mission (RGNDWM) on 1:50,000 scale using 23 M resolution satellite data of year 2006 and groundwater quality using 2013-2018 pre monsoon/post monsoon water sample. This project was sponsored by Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), Government of India. MRSAC and GSDA were the partner institutes for preparation of groundwater prospect maps and water quality maps for entire state of Maharashtra. Groundwater prospect database is being shared with many departments of government of Maharashtra for rural development. MRSAC has started 10K Mapping for Natural resources and assets under MAhaBHUMI project. Base map generation is in progress for Maharashtra under MahaBHUMI project wherein the mapping is being carried out on 2K and publishing the topomaps on 10K. Using these 10K Basemap, Geology and Geomorphology shall be interpreted.

The objective of the present study is to update the on 1:50,000 scale Groundwater prospects maps, prepared under NRDWP to a detailed 1:10,000 scale maps using VHR satellite data. The maps can be utilized for further narrowing down the prospective zones for groundwater and to suggest suitable sites for recharge structure.

These maps will be generated with the following objectives:

1. To generate the thematic layers of influential factors using very high resolution satellite data on 1:10,000 scale for up scaling the groundwater prospects map with reference to existing 1:50,000 scale groundwater prospect mapping data prepared under Rajeev Gandhi National Drinking Water Mission (RGNDWM).

2. To incorporate the field data pertaining to well inventory, well yield and quality of groundwater for enhancing the resolution of the resultant groundwater prospects map.

3. To integrate the available data (MRSAC) of existing water and soil conservation structures with groundwater prospects map for optimizing groundwater recharge planning.

4. To integrate all groundwater related thematic layers and database generated from field investigations to generate the village wise groundwater prospects and recharge priority maps.
